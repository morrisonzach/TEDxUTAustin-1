Hypatia Sorunke

producer/director
she/her
  • bio
  • link(s)

A multimedia artist based in Austin, Texas. Originally a movement artist and fiction writer, she has been able to combine her passions of storytelling and visual art through her photography, cinematography, and writing. As a recent graduate from the University of Texas at Austin with a Plan II Honors and African and African Diaspora Studies dual degree with a minor in Government, she is interested in understanding identity politics as it surrounds queer people of color, with a primary focus on Black queer women, and creating interactive pieces that provide a social commentary on the world. Her artistic approach is best summed up by the Franz Fanon quote,“ I am not a prisoner of History… In the world I am heading for, I am endlessly creating myself,” from Black Skin, White Masks. She often revisits this mantra to ground herself in the endless possibilities she imagines of the future, as her artistic process is like a child on a playground, where imagination leads and the body follows.

Malik Julien

cast
he/him
  • bio
  • link(s)

Recently turned 20, Malik Julien is a young independent filmmaker who is taking on his third year at The University of Texas at Austin where he’s studying Radio-Television-Film and pursuing a double minor in Black Studies and Entrepreneurship. Julien is a Black, Queer, and Christian identifying artist who works towards telling the untold stories of the Black and Queer communities. Julien grew up in a small coastal town by the name of Texas City, TX where he was raised by his Mother and Grandfather. Now located in Austin, He’s just recently made his directorial debut with the upcoming documentary, And They Were Loved, a story about three queer individuals navigating their path towards self-identity while facing the challenges of being queer in the American South. Julien says that Ballroom was a driving force for this documentary and it has opened a whirlwind of life-changing opportunities for myself and for many involved. “Ballroom offered a piece of life that my young queer self had always longed for.” For Julien, Ballroom is a haven for self-expression, acceptance, & most of all, family. The Legendary House of Blahnik opened their doors to me early 2020 and I’m beyond excited to what the future will hold,” Julien says.

My name is Leo Baldwin, I am 22 years old and I am from the illustrious House of Baldwin. When I first encountered vogue I instantly fell in love. Vogue helps me express myself creatively by telling a story. The category I am going to practice for is butch queen fem and my favorite element is hands. I look up to icons like Makayla Lanvin and Leiomy Maldonado because of their contribution to this community. Seeing them dance so passionately motivated me to pursue my dream of becoming a polished voguer.

Prince Baldwin

I am 22 years old born and raised in Dallas Tx currently enrolled as a senior at Texas State University majoring in Computer science. I’ve been voguing for about 2 months now gaining inspiration from amazingly talented femme queens such as Daesja Laperla may she Rest In Peace, Leiomy, Tamiyah, Starr Revlon and Sinia Braxton. Voguing helps me release a certain type of energy that I don’t usually release on the daily basis. I’m a very chill person who tends to be more introverted, but when I step on that dance floor all of that goes away. When I vogue I feel sexy and untamable, when I vogue I feel like the little boy inside of me is finally getting to express himself. When I vogue I feel love.
